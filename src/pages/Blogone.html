AI for Space Exploration

Space is a mysterious place; from an early age, humans have tried to understand what is beyond the sky and learn about the secrets within the universe. In the Twenty-first century, data scientists and programmers design and develop machine learning artificial intelligence that requires intense computing and coding. These AI's can assist scientists in uncovering the secrets and understanding how the universe works. Network expert Ashesh Anand explained that "Artificial intelligence (AI) assists astronauts on their strenuous space trips and assists in the execution of space tasks that would be impossible if only human capabilities were used" (Anand). Artificial intelligence can benefit space exploration by helping with complex mathematical equations, providing medical support to astronauts, and automating spacecraft. When trying to solve complex mathematical problems, humans cannot simultaneously work with different equations at high speed, but with artificial intelligence, the mathematical problems can be solved at a much higher speed and accuracy. Space exploration requires intense math, and with the help of AI, humans can solve tricky equations, making space exploration faster and more accurate. Robert Sanders, manager of science at UC Berkeley, states that "AI, also called machine learning, can reveal something deeper, University of California, Berkeley, astronomers found: unsuspected connections hidden in the complex mathematics arising from general relativity -- in particular, how that theory is applied to finding new planets around other stars." (Sanders). Artificial intelligence can impact space exploration in various ways; it can be used to solve linear equations for finding distance and interception points or predicting the outcomes. In addition, scientists can use artificial intelligence to determine the distance between stars and predict movements that are going to happen within our solar system. As a result, space exploration will be much faster and more accurate with the help of AI. Artificial intelligence can be smarter than humans, and without human intervention, they can figure out how to help living things by providing life support. For example, AI can support astronauts with their medical needs. NASA's AI consultant, Graham Mackintosh suggests, "With plans for long-duration human missions to distant places like the planet Mars coming into focus, some experts at NASA and private space companies like SpaceX are considering the potential for automated diagnosis, treatment—and even prediction—of in-space ailments" Mackintosh). AI can help astronauts with their medical needs, such as monthly checkups or treatments, and also stop human intervention, which reduces the margin of error of getting sick. Therefore, space exploration would be much faster and more efficient because there would be less sickness among the astronauts. With the help of AI, probes and spacecraft can learn how to work autonomously and transmit what they discovered without human intervention. Probes and spacecraft are essential pieces of technology used to explore deep space. Scientists use spacecraft or probes to examine the universe and conduct research that help them gain new pieces of information and understand how the universe works. The European Space Agency suggests, "Using these operations (AI), the spacecraft itself can perform autonomous replanning, detect events (both internal and external), and react accordingly, ensuring fulfillment of mission objectives without the delays introduced by the decision-making process on the ground" (ai-solutions ESA). AI-powered probes and spacecraft can help scientists by traveling alone in space and explaining what has been learned. For example, AI-powered probes can travel quickly and take pictures while maneuvering around planets and asteroids. Artificial intelligence probes and spacecraft can benefit space exploration because they will lower the costs of space exploration and, most importantly, save time. Artificial intelligence can open many doors and buy time; however, it has limitations and side effects. For example, artificial intelligence does not have emotional intelligence. With today's technology, it is nearly impossible to make AI develop emotional intelligence; therefore, with the lack of emotional intelligence, they cannot understand the behavior of lifeforms. However, AI's are developed to help with logical and mathematical problems and do not need to partake in emotional intelligence because humans are far more emotionally advanced. Electricity is vital to artificial intelligence, such as computers, and since electricity is finite, AI cannot operate without them. With the power of solar panels, electricity will be infinite until the source, which is the sun dies. AI will also bring danger because people can use AI to harm each other with bombs and gadgets. Artificial intelligence can navigate bombs and gadgets to hit targets without relying on human calculations; however, it is possible to avoid these problems with the correct usage of AI; for example, scientists can use artificial intelligence for the benefit of humanity and advance in exploring the universe rather than harming people. AI will help in many branches of science, including math, physics, and biology, which are essential in space exploration. In addition, AI can solve complex equations in seconds and help humanity with their medical needs. In the twenty-first century, humans are advancing beyond Earth and looking for secrets in space with the help of technology, and with the introduction of AI, space exploration is getting more accessible than ever before and changing how humans perceive the universe.
